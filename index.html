Generation of Synthetic Datasets of Highway Driving Scenarios

Abstract

Driving scenario recognition is crucial for vehicle context adaptivity, decision-making, and automated driving function validation. However, research in this area is limited by a critical shortage of publicly available, scenario-labeled video datasets. To bridge this gap, we propose a system for generating scenario-labeled synthetic video clips from statistical distributions of traffic datapoints. Leveraging the large-scale, real-world, proprietary MOOVE collection, we generated and publicly released a 9-class dataset featuring varied highway conditions. Based on our experience, key insights for efficient synthetic dataset creation include the necessity of a rigorous scenario-modeling methodology, for which OpenSCENARIO 2.0 was effective, and the need to enrich a simulator like CARLA with behaviors for enhanced realism and scalability to produce thousands of samples for each class. Exploiting the synthesized dataset, we developed a classification framework based on state-of-the-art DL architectures. This approach enabled, to our knowledge, the first documented real-time recognition of a significant set of highway driving scenarios, establishing a novel benchmark in the field. Furthermore, preliminary results to validate the proposed approach are promising: models trained on our synthetically generated videos can effectively process real-world data in real-time. This method, parameterized solely by statistical distributions, leverages a synthesis of field data to create shareable datasets. Thus, it expands the open-source research toolkit, offering significant advantages for both academy and industry by enabling efficient pre-training on synthetic data before fine-tuning on proprietary data for final deployment.
