Abstract

Driving scenario recognition is a higher order perception task that is getting ever more importance for vehicle context adaptivity, maneuver decision making and validation & verification of automated driving functions (ADFs). However, despite the abundancy of high-quality perception datasets, there is a lack of publicly available video datasets with driving scenario labels. Since simulations are a key asset for AD system development, we propose a system for generating scenario-labeled video-clips from scenario-description distributions. Utilizing the tool, and exploiting configuration data from a large-scale real-world collection, we have built a 9-class dataset, that features a variety of highway driving conditions (trajectories, traffic, weather, time of day) and we release it publicly at: XXX. Results from a classification pipeline that we built exploiting state of the art deep learning models, also including semantic pre-processing, indicate that the task is challenging and should stimulate research on new models, better able to capture the nuances among the patterns characterizing the different classes. From our experience developing the system, we highlight three main factors for efficient creation of synthetic datasets. First, there is a need for a rigorous methodology for modeling scenarios, for which we resorted to Asam OpenSCENARIO 2.0. Second, we needed to enrich a state-of-the-art AD-research simulator, such as CARLA, particularly with behaviors aimed at increasing realism and flexibility, given the need to generate several samples for each scenario type. Finally, simulations should be grounded on meaningful data, that our system uses to configure the dataset creation. We believe that this work can open significant perspectives for future research by extending the proposed approach, improving state of the art scenario recognition models and supporting real-world system development through pre-training, co-training, and fine tuning.
